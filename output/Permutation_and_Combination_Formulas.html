Permutation and Combination Formulas

1. Permutations with Constraints

  • Linear: \( n! \)
  • With repetitions: \( \frac{n!}{k1! \times k2! \times \cdots} \)
  • Circular: \( (n-1)! \)
  • Circular with reflection: \( \frac{(n-1)!}{2} \)
  • Grouping: Treat group as unit, multiply by internal arrangements.
  • Not together: Total - together.
  • Example: Arrange 3 boys, 2 girls (girls together): \( 4! \times 2! = 48 \).

    • 2. Combinations with Constraints

  • Basic: \( \binom{n}{r} = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!} \)
  • At least one: Total - none.
  • Exactly one: \( \binom{n}{1} \times \binom{m}{r-1} \).
  • Example: 2 numbers, at least one prime: \( \binom{10}{2} - \binom{6}{2} = 30 \).

    • 3. Counting Paths or Sequences

  • Independent choices: \( a \times b \times \cdots \)
  • Grid paths: \( \binom{n+k}{n} \)
  • At least one: \( 2^n - 1 \)
  • Generating functions: Coefficient of \( x^k \).
  • Example: Paths to (5, 6): \( \binom{11}{5} = 462 \).

    • 4. Number Formation

  • With repetition: \( a \times b \times \cdots \)
  • No repetition: \( P(n,r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!} \)
  • No leading zero: Adjust first digit.
  • Example: 3-digit even numbers: Casework yields 36.

    • 5. Assignment Problems

  • Distinct: \( n! \)
  • Multiple: \( n^m \)
  • Restrictions: Total - invalid.
  • Example: 4 tasks, 1st person not 1st task: \( 4! - 3! = 18 \).

    • 6. Geometric Counting

  • Lines: \( \binom{n}{2} \)
  • Rectangles: \( \binom{r+1}{2} \times \binom{c+1}{2} \)
  • Squares: \( \sum (r+1-k)(c+1-k) \)
  • Example: 8Ã—8 rectangles: \( \binom{9}{2}^2 = 1296 \).

    • 7. Handshake or Pairing

  • Handshakes: \( \binom{n}{2} \)
  • Example: 55 handshakes: \( \binom{11}{2} = 55 \).

    • 8. Miscellaneous Counting

  • Factors: \( (e1+1)(e2+1) \cdots \)
  • Perfect squares: Product of \( \lfloor e_i/2 \rfloor + 1 \).
  • Digit counts: Positional analysis.
  • Example: Perfect squares of \( 2^5 \times 3^6 \times 5^2 \): \( 3 \times 4 \times 2 = 24 \).
